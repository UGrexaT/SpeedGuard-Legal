Privacy Policy for SpeedGuard

Effective Date: April 9, 2026

Revaz Ugrekhelidze ("we," "us," or "our") built SpeedGuard as a Freemium app. This Service is provided at no cost for the basic tier and is intended for use as is.

This page describes our policies regarding the collection, use, and disclosure of personal information when you use SpeedGuard. By using the app, you agree to these practices.

1. Information We Collect and How We Use It

We only collect the minimum data necessary to provide core functionality.

A. Location Data (Foreground and Background)

SpeedGuard is a real-time speedometer and speed limit alert application. To function, it requires access to your device's GPS location.

Foreground Location — collected while the app is open to:

  • Calculate your current speed
  • Determine the road you are driving on
  • Display the posted speed limit

Background Location — collected when the app is closed or not in active use to:

  • Provide continuous speed monitoring
  • Deliver real-time audio alerts if you exceed the speed limit

Background location access is essential to SpeedGuard's core functionality. Without it, the app cannot provide safety alerts while driving with the screen off or while using other apps.

How Location Data Is Processed:

  • Location data is processed in real time
  • Short location traces are temporarily transmitted to routing providers (Valhalla and/or HERE Maps) to determine speed limits
  • This data is not stored on our servers and is not linked to your identity

B. Usage and Diagnostics Data

We may collect non-personal diagnostic data such as crash reports, device model, operating system version, and performance metrics. This data is used solely to improve app stability and performance.

C. Advertising Data (Free Tier Only)

For users on the free tier, SpeedGuard uses Google AdMob to display advertisements. This service may collect advertising identifiers (e.g., Android Advertising ID) and ad interaction data to serve personalized or non-personalized ads.

You can opt out of personalized advertising at any time through your device settings (Settings → Google → Ads → "Opt out of Ads Personalization" on Android).

2. Legal Basis for Processing

If you are located in a region with data protection laws such as the GDPR (EEA, UK), we process your data based on:

  • Consent — for location tracking and personalized ads
  • Performance of a contract — to provide speed monitoring and alerts
  • Legitimate interests — to improve app performance, reliability, and safety features

3. Third-Party Services

SpeedGuard relies on trusted third-party services. These providers may process limited data strictly for functionality purposes:

  • Google Play Services — core Android functionality and location services
  • Google AdMob — advertising for free-tier users
  • Google Play Billing — secure subscription and purchase processing
  • HERE Maps / Valhalla — speed limit and road matching services
  • Firebase (Crashlytics & Analytics) — crash reporting and usage analytics

These providers are obligated to use your data only for the services they provide. We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal data to third parties.

4. Data Retention and Security

Location Data: processed in real time, not stored on our servers, and not retained after use.

Local Data: app preferences (units, alert settings, subscription status) are stored locally on your device.

No method of electronic transmission or storage is 100% secure. While we take reasonable measures to protect your information, we cannot guarantee absolute security.

5. Your Privacy Rights

Depending on your location, you may have the right to:

  • Access the data we process about you
  • Request deletion of your data
  • Withdraw consent at any time (e.g., by disabling location permissions)
  • Opt out of personalized advertising

Because SpeedGuard does not store personal location data on its servers, most data can be removed by uninstalling the app or clearing app storage on your device.

6. Children's Privacy

SpeedGuard is not directed at anyone under the age of 13. We do not knowingly collect personally identifiable information from children under 13. If we become aware that a child has provided personal data, we will delete it immediately. If you are a parent or guardian and believe your child has provided us with information, please contact us.
