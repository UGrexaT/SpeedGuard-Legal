SpeedGuard Privacy Policy

Effective date: May 8, 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how SpeedGuard ("SpeedGuard," "we," "us," or "our") handles information when you use the SpeedGuard mobile app.

Developer / legal entity: Revaz Ugrekhelidze

Privacy contact: speedguardapp@gmail.com

Summary

SpeedGuard is a driving aid that uses your device location to measure speed, look up posted speed limits, and alert you when you exceed your configured threshold. SpeedGuard does not sell personal data or sensitive personal data.

Depending on how you use the app, SpeedGuard may process:

  • Device location and driving-related readings while monitoring is active.
  • Location-derived road lookup requests sent to SpeedGuard services and mapping providers.
  • Local settings, monitoring-session state, short last-known speed-limit state, trusted premium speed-limit cache entries, and debug logs.
  • App diagnostics, crash reports, and analytics after onboarding is completed, if enabled for the app build and permitted by your settings or applicable consent choices.
  • Advertising identifiers or ad-related data for free-tier users if ads are enabled.
  • Subscription and purchase entitlement information through Google Play and RevenueCat.

Information We Collect or Process

Location and Driving Data

SpeedGuard accesses device location when you grant location permission and start monitoring. This may include:

  • Latitude and longitude.
  • GPS accuracy or radius.
  • Heading.
  • Speed or speed-derived readings.
  • Timestamps associated with location updates.
  • Short recent GPS traces used to match your position to a road segment.

We use this data to:

  • Show your current speed.
  • Look up posted speed limits for your current road.
  • Determine whether you are within, near, or above the posted speed limit.
  • Trigger in-app, audio, vibration, and notification alerts.
  • Announce speed limits when enabled in your settings.
  • Keep monitoring active through an Android foreground service after you intentionally start monitoring.

SpeedGuard may continue processing location while the app is minimized or the screen is off, but only after you start monitoring. Monitoring can be stopped from the app or the foreground-service notification.

Speed-Limit Lookup Requests

To identify posted speed limits, SpeedGuard sends location-derived road lookup data to SpeedGuard backend services. These requests may include:

  • A short GPS trace containing latitude, longitude, heading, GPS accuracy, and timestamps.
  • A generated install token in the x-device-token header, when available, for coarse diagnostics, rate-limiting, and abuse prevention.
  • API authentication or app-attestation headers required by SpeedGuard services.
  • Basic request metadata such as app version, request time, and network information.

SpeedGuard's speed-limit lookup uses a SpeedGuard gateway and self-hosted Valhalla map-matching backend. For premium users, SpeedGuard may use HERE Maps speed-limit lookup to retrieve a posted limit for the current road area. HERE speed-limit values may be treated as authoritative for premium speed-limit display and alerts.

These services process location-derived road data to return speed-limit information. SpeedGuard does not use precise foreground or background location data for advertising targeting.

Local App Data

SpeedGuard stores some data locally on your device using app storage, including:

  • Your settings, such as speed unit, alert threshold, alert style, volume, speed-limit announcement preferences, and feature toggles.
  • Onboarding completion state.
  • Active monitoring-session state used to recover or continue monitoring safely.
  • Short last-known or held speed-limit state used during transient lookup failures.
  • Premium trusted HERE speed-limit cache entries, when available, so premium lookups can remain reliable during short network or provider delays.
  • A random install token used for gateway diagnostics and rate-limiting.
  • Your last-known subscription tier.
  • Debug logs stored in an on-device ring buffer.

SpeedGuard no longer uses a generic free-tier segment cache for normal live monitoring. The app may still keep short session state, held last-known values, and premium trusted cache entries where needed for reliability.

Local app data is generally removed when you clear app data or uninstall SpeedGuard, subject to Android platform behavior and any device backup settings.

Debug Logs

SpeedGuard includes an in-app debug log to help diagnose driving-session behavior. Debug logs may include timestamps, speed-limit lookup results, speed and alert decisions, GPS accuracy, provider status, road names or route labels, and similar diagnostic information.

Debug logs are stored locally on your device in a limited-size buffer. They are not sent to SpeedGuard unless you choose to export or share them, or unless similar information is separately included in crash reports or backend service logs.

Diagnostics, Analytics, and Crash Reports

After onboarding is complete, SpeedGuard may initialize Firebase Analytics and Firebase Crashlytics if enabled for the app build. These services may process:

  • App events, such as monitoring start or stop, screen views, purchase flow events, and feature usage.
  • Crash reports, non-fatal error reports, logs, and diagnostic metadata.
  • App version, device model, operating system version, and similar technical information.
  • Subscription tier as an analytics property or crash-report attribute.

We use this information to understand app quality, diagnose crashes, improve reliability, and measure feature usage. We do not intentionally include precise route history in analytics events.

Advertising Data

Free-tier users may see banner ads through Google AdMob if advertising is enabled and production ad IDs are configured. Google AdMob and related Google advertising services may process device identifiers, advertising identifiers, approximate location, IP address, ad interaction data, and other data as described by Google's policies.

Ads are not shown to premium users. SpeedGuard does not use precise foreground or background location data for ad targeting.

Where required by law or platform policy, including for users in the European Economic Area, the United Kingdom, or Switzerland, SpeedGuard or Google may ask for consent choices before using cookies, local storage, advertising identifiers, analytics storage, or personalized ads.

Subscription and Purchase Data

SpeedGuard uses RevenueCat and Google Play Billing to manage subscriptions and lifetime purchases. Purchase processing is handled by Google Play and RevenueCat. SpeedGuard may receive or process:

  • RevenueCat customer information and entitlement status.
  • Product identifiers for monthly, yearly, or lifetime access.
  • Purchase, restore, customer-center, and paywall result events.
  • Subscription tier used to unlock premium features.

SpeedGuard does not receive your full payment card number from Google Play or RevenueCat.

Network and Device Information

When the app communicates with SpeedGuard services or third-party SDKs, basic network and device information may be processed automatically, such as:

  • IP address.
  • App version.
  • Device and operating system information.
  • Request timestamps.
  • Service logs required for security, diagnostics, rate-limiting, abuse prevention, and service reliability.

How We Use Information

We use information to:

  • Provide real-time speed monitoring and speed-limit alerts.
  • Run background monitoring after you start it.
  • Fetch posted speed limits and verify speed-limit changes.
  • Maintain app settings, monitoring state, and subscription state.
  • Provide free-tier advertising and premium no-ad access.
  • Process purchases and restore entitlements.
  • Diagnose crashes, bugs, map-matching failures, and service failures.
  • Protect the service from abuse, enforce rate limits, and maintain security.
  • Comply with legal obligations and Google Play requirements.

How We Share Information

We may share information with service providers that help operate SpeedGuard, including:

  • SpeedGuard backend infrastructure for speed-limit lookup, diagnostics, rate-limiting, and security.
  • Self-hosted Valhalla-backed map-matching services operated for SpeedGuard.
  • HERE Maps for premium speed-limit lookup.
  • Firebase / Google for analytics and crash reporting.
  • Google AdMob for ads shown to free-tier users.
  • Google Play Billing for app purchases and subscriptions.
  • RevenueCat for purchase, subscription, entitlement, paywall, and customer-center features.

These providers process data according to their own terms and privacy policies and/or as service providers for app functionality. We may also disclose information if required by law, to protect rights and safety, or as part of a merger, acquisition, or business transfer.

We do not sell personal data or sensitive personal data.

Permissions

SpeedGuard may request:

  • Location permission, including precise and approximate location access needed for monitoring and speed-limit lookup.
  • Background or foreground-service location access where required for monitoring after you start it.
  • Notification permission on Android 13 and later so the app can show the required foreground monitoring notification and monitoring alerts.
  • Vibration permission for alerts.
  • Internet access for speed-limit lookups, purchases, ads, diagnostics, and service communication.

You can manage permissions in Android system settings. If required permissions are disabled, monitoring may not work.

  • Consent, where required for location permissions, background location access, notifications, analytics, advertising storage, personalized ads, or similar choices.

Data Retention

Local settings, monitoring state, debug logs, held last-known speed-limit state, premium trusted speed-limit cache entries, and the install token are retained on your device until they are overwritten by app operation, cleared by you, or removed when you clear app data or uninstall SpeedGuard.

Debug logs are kept in a limited-size local buffer and older entries are overwritten as new entries are added.

SpeedGuard backend logs and diagnostics are retained only as long as reasonably needed for service operation, security, debugging, rate-limiting, abuse prevention, and legal compliance.

Firebase, AdMob, Google Play, RevenueCat, HERE, and other providers may retain data according to their own policies and your account, device, consent, or privacy settings.

You can:

  • Stop monitoring at any time from the app or the foreground-service notification.
  • Disable app permissions in Android settings.
  • Change speed-limit announcement and alert settings in the app.
  • Clear exported debug logs before sharing them, or choose not to share them.
  • Disable ads by purchasing a premium tier, if available.
  • Clear local app data or uninstall SpeedGuard to remove local settings, monitoring state, local logs, and local tokens from your device.
  • Use Google Play controls and Google account settings for purchase, ad, and privacy choices where applicable.
  • Contact us to request privacy assistance.

Automated Decisions

SpeedGuard automatically compares your current speed against the current posted speed limit to display status and trigger alerts. This driving-aid functionality does not make decisions that produce legal or similarly significant effects about you.
